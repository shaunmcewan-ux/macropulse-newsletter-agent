This Week

The week the Fed shows its hand

Five data points matter this week, and three of them land before Wednesday. Tuesday’s CPI is the headline event — consensus sits at 2.8% year over year, and any print below 2.7 puts a June cut firmly back on the table. The market is currently pricing 38%.

“The dial sits at +3.0 because the conditions for a sustained risk-on tape are present — not because the all-clear has been given.”

Wednesday’s FOMC minutes are the secondary tell. The April meeting was unanimous on hold, but the post-meeting press conference left room for dissent. If the minutes show three or more participants flagging cuts, the long end of the curve moves first — and altcoins move second.

Thursday’s NVDA print is the wildcard. A beat-and-raise extends the AI capex narrative through Q3 and pulls liquidity back into mega-cap tech. A miss — even a small one — sends rotation money into the names that have been compressing for three months.
