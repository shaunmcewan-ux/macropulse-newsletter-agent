MacroPulse
Homepage v6 · Direction D, 4 layouts
Internal · 11 May 2026
Design Review · Pass 6

A homepage that refreshes weekly even when the regime doesn't.

v5's Ledger shipped well visually, but its proposition rests on volume of entries. With cycle states holding for months at a time, the homepage spends long stretches looking like a thin list. Direction D replaces the Ledger's spine with three sources that update on their own clocks — this Tuesday's issue, the live macro tape, the next fortnight's calendar — and keeps the cycle state visible as a kicker rather than the page's organising principle.

Inputs: v5 live · field observation Brief: substance every Tuesday, regime-agnostic System: locked

The diagnosis

The Ledger says "look at the record" and the record is the page. That works on the Tuesday a new state ships. It works less well in the 16 Tuesdays between. State changes are rare; the page is loaded daily. The arithmetic of substance per visit breaks down.

The pivot

Build the homepage around the three things that already change weekly: the latest issue's lede, the eight-indicator macro evidence (daily bot), and the next fourteen days of macro events (same bot). The cycle state stays a prominent kicker; "0 revisions" retreats to a credibility line, not the spine.

The test that every layout must pass

State has been "BTC Accumulation" for twelve months. The latest issue shipped Tuesday. Three indicators ticked overnight. Four events sit in the next fortnight. Does the page feel full — not busy, full — to a returning reader on the 51st Tuesday? If yes, ship. If no, try again.

I

Four layouts on Direction D.

Same three substance sources in every layout. What changes is which one leads, how they nest, where the cycle state and the "0 revisions" track-record claim end up. Each has wires for 390px mobile and 1180px desktop, a one-paragraph theory, the trade-offs, and the result of the twelfth-month test.

Above the fold reads as a Tuesday morning newspaper: a kicker identifies the regime, a display headline carries the lede, a right rail holds the macro tape. Decisions
Above-fold lead
Issue headline + opening 2–3 paragraphs. The week's writing carries the top.
Composition
Side-by-side desktop (60% prose / 40% tape). Stacked mobile, issue first.
Cycle state
Kicker line above the headline. Same mono dot pattern as v4's eyebrow.
"0 revisions"
Footer-adjacent line: "Ledger · 25 calls, 0 revised since Nov 2024", linking to /ledger/.
Visual moves
Display headline owns the page
Fraunces clamp 40–64px on a sentence written for Tuesday. The single loudest object. Cycle state lives quietly above it; everything else lives quietly to its right or below.
Tape as a right rail
A 280-wide vertical strip — eight rows, mono, tabular, with a one-line prose summary at the foot. Reads as a wire-service tape pinned beside the article.
Calendar as a quiet table
Four columns of mono: date · event · sub-spec · time. No card, no glow, no icons. The week-ahead as a printed schedule.
Track-record demoted to a sign-off
"Ledger · 25 calls, 0 revised" sits in the CTA strip beside the subscribe link. Still a credibility signal; no longer trying to be the spine.
Twelfth-month test
Passes. The headline is new every Tuesday, so the page's loudest object refreshes weekly regardless of state. Tape and calendar tick on their own clocks. The risk lives only in which week's lede the page caught — a thin issue produces a thin homepage.
Trade-offs vs. v5 Ledger

Better

  • The page is substantive every Tuesday by construction.
  • Lowest build cost of the four — almost no new components.
  • SEO upside: real editorial content on the homepage, weekly.
  • Works equally well at month 3 of a state and month 13.

Worse

  • If a week's lede is weak, the homepage is weak. No buffer.
  • Risk of duplicate-content with /issue/47/.
  • The cycle state — formerly the brand's load-bearing wall — is now a kicker. Some equity is lost.
  • "Showing the latest writing" is itself a marketing pattern; can drift into theatre.
Marketing-page risk
MediumThe form looks editorial, but "always show the freshest content above the fold" is the move every marketing site makes. Mitigated by never tuning the lede for the homepage — whatever Tuesday says, that's what's shown.
Effort
Low · ~1 dayPipe latest issue's first 200 words + headline + byline into the homepage at publish. Tape and calendar render from the bot's two JSON files.

The page opens with a horizontal tape — a single mono row, wrapped to two visual lines on desktop, containing the cycle token, the eight indicators, and a one-line prose read. The macro state is part of the tape, not separate from it. Below: lede in editorial type at left, calendar dates in mono at right.

Decisions
Above-fold lead
Eight-indicator tape, full width. The macro picture is the page's first impression.
Composition
Tape stacked above a two-column body (prose left, calendar right).
Cycle state
Inline as the tape's leftmost token: "BTC ACCUM · W7". Reads as one indicator among many.
"0 revisions"
Footer line beside the subscribe CTA, linking to /ledger/. Same line as in D1.
Visual moves
Cycle state inside the tape
Demoting "BTC Accumulation" from page-headline to token zero of the tape is the radical move. It positions the regime as derived from the indicators — not asserted. Most defensible posture available.
Tape wraps to two lines
A single mono line, wrapped — not two separate strips. Gives the surface the rhythm of a wire-service printout. Hairline rule between wraps acts as the carriage-return mark.
Prose summary line under the tape
One italic Fraunces sentence — "Three ticked overnight. Dollar still the story." — translates the eight rows into a human read. Editorial gesture inside a data block.
Calendar in margin
Right-rail datelines, each a two-line block (date + event/time). Lighter typographic weight than the prose, but same vertical rhythm — reads as "what's on the radio next."
Twelfth-month test
Passes — strongest of the four. The tape ticks daily, the lede ships weekly, the calendar refreshes continuously. None of the three depends on the state changing. Even on a Tuesday when "Accumulation, Week 51" stares back from the top-left token, seven other tokens around it carry the load.
Trade-offs vs. v5 Ledger

Better

  • Substance arrives from the data, not from claim or copy.
  • The cycle state earns its place by being shown alongside its evidence.
  • Tape doubles as a brand asset — visually distinctive, hard to copy.
  • Robust against a slow news week.

Worse

  • Densest above-the-fold of the four — closest to dashboard-lite if discipline slips.
  • The tape's typographic rhythm is fragile; one wrong width breaks the wire-service feel.
  • Risks demoting the cycle state too far — first-time visitors may not parse it.
  • Tape needs an empty-state when the bot is mid-update (rare but possible).
Marketing-page risk
LowA page that opens with eight tabular numbers cannot drift into marketing language. The page is shaped by its data discipline.
Effort
Medium · ~2 daysTape composition + responsive wrap is the lift. Bot data already exists.

Three columns, three registers: editorial Fraunces on the left for the writing; mono tabular in the centre for the evidence; mono dateline blocks on the right for the calendar. The columns share only a kicker bar across the top. The "broadsheet" gesture is that each column is shaped by what it carries — they don't pretend to be the same shape, the way three identical cards on a marketing page do.

Decisions
Above-fold lead
All three, equally weighted. A broadsheet front page, not a hero.
Composition
Three columns desktop (roughly 40/30/30). Stacks I → II → III on mobile.
Cycle state
Standalone kicker spanning all three columns. A masthead bar, not a chip.
"0 revisions"
Footer-adjacent: "Maintained since Nov 2024 · 0 revised", linked to /ledger/.
Visual moves
Roman-numeral column heads
I · This week's issue, II · The evidence, III · The fortnight. A small typographic gesture that recasts the homepage as a three-act publication, not a stack of sections. Costs four characters per column, adds register.
Three typographic registers
Serif body in column I; mono tabular in column II; mono dateline blocks in column III. Each column looks like the kind of substance it is. This is the page's main defence against feeling like marketing.
Kicker as masthead bar
A single line of mono spanning the full container, dot + cycle state + week + held-since. Double-rule beneath separates kicker from columns — the only place the design borrows a print-broadsheet device literally.
Same publish discipline as a newspaper
Three sources, each maintained on its own clock. If one is empty (a quiet calendar week), the column self-narrows; the others fill. No empty hero.
Twelfth-month test
Passes. Three things refresh independently of the cycle state. Even if the kicker bar says "BTC Accumulation · Week 51", the three columns beneath carry the page. The kicker becomes the page's only static element — and the only one whose stability is itself the argument.
Trade-offs vs. v5 Ledger

Better

  • Most balanced — no single source carries the weight.
  • The "broadsheet" frame is brand-distinctive; nobody else does this.
  • Failure-graceful: any column can be thin without the page failing.
  • The cycle state regains prominence by being a masthead, not a body element.

Worse

  • Highest mobile-collapse cost — three columns become a long stack.
  • The roman-numeral gesture risks reading as fussy if the columns are unbalanced.
  • Three columns at 1180px means each column is narrow (~350px); typography has to be tuned tight.
  • Needs the calendar to be non-empty most weeks; quiet weeks pull a column down.
Marketing-page risk
LowThree columns of distinct registers can't easily be hijacked by a marketing voice. The form does the discipline.
Effort
Medium · ~2–3 daysThree-column desktop + clean mobile stack + the typographic register work per column. Bot data already exists for II and III.

One column. Editorial body, interrupted by mono pulls.

Issue lede runs as one editorial column down the centre of the page; between paragraphs, mono indicator pulls and forthcoming-date pulls appear like marginal notes that have been folded inline. The three substance sources read as one continuous Tuesday briefing rather than three blocks.

Decisions
Above-fold lead
Issue lede — same as D1, but interrupted, not flanked.
Composition
Single editorial column with mono pulls at a tighter indent between paragraphs.
Cycle state
Kicker line above headline — as D1.
"0 revisions"
Footer line beside subscribe CTA, linked to /ledger/.
Visual moves
Pulls chosen to match the paragraph
This is the surprise. Each mono pull is selected at publish time to be the data the paragraph above it is talking about. The page reads as if the indicators are footnotes inside the prose — evidence shown the moment it's claimed.
Orange tick rule on every pull
The 24px orange-2 tick that's already the system's hairline-rule device repurposed as a pull marker. Re-uses an existing component instead of inventing.
"See the rest" hand-offs
Below the article, two single-line links: "See the full tape (8 indicators)" and "See the fortnight (4 more dates)". The page commits to four pulls inline; the rest is one click away. Discipline against bloat.
Narrow editorial measure
~560px column on a 1180px page. The wide outer margins are what make the inline pulls feel like marginal notes, not table inserts.
Twelfth-month test
Passes — most editorially. Because each pull is chosen per issue, the page is the issue. State doesn't enter the question. The fragility is the inverse of D1's: it depends not on the lede being strong, but on the writer (or a small editor tool) picking three to four good pulls per Tuesday. That is a real weekly cost the bot can't cover.
Trade-offs vs. v5 Ledger

Better

  • Most editorially original of the four. Strong "publication" identity.
  • Solves "feels basic" with a typographic gesture, not more components.
  • Reads as one continuous artefact, not three glued blocks.
  • Mobile collapse is graceful — interleaving still works at 390px.

Worse

  • Requires a curator. Pulls don't pick themselves; writer must mark them per issue.
  • If pulls are auto-picked badly, the device fails worse than not having it at all.
  • Demotes the calendar to a footer afterthought.
  • Hardest of the four to A/B against v5 — the gesture is the whole point.
Marketing-page risk
MediumA page that reads as one editorial artefact is closer to "pitch deck" than D2 or D3. Mitigation: the pulls must be data, not blurbs — no testimonial, no "what readers say" allowed in a pull.
Effort
Medium · ~2 days build + weekly curationBuild is small. The ongoing cost is the writer's per-issue selection of 3–4 pulls — call it 10 minutes a Tuesday.
II

Three alternates for the cycle-state kicker.

The current line — "NOW · BTC Accumulation, 7 weeks held." — is functional but flat. Three voices across the tonal range, each ~6–12 words, each layout-independent: drop any into any of the four directions above.

Currentbaseline
NOW · BTC Accumulation, 7 weeks held.
Functional. Reads as the field label it is: "current value, duration." Acceptable; uninteresting.
01 · Quietly factualwire-service register
CURRENT CALL · BTC ACCUMULATION · HELD SINCE TUE 25 MAR 2026
Same posture as the dashboard's tape line. Drops the weeks-count in favour of the start date — readers can do the subtraction themselves, and the absolute date is a stronger claim than a counter that ticks every week.
02 · Slightly literaryeditorial register
The reading has been accumulation since the dollar last blinked.
Italic Fraunces, not mono. The kicker becomes a sentence — quietly literary, slightly poetic. Works particularly well in D4, where the kicker already wants to feel editorial. Costs a small amount of legibility for a large amount of voice.
03 · Brutally shortheadline register
ACCUMULATION. WEEK 7.
Three words. Two periods. The line stops being a label and starts being a statement. Pairs best with D2 (where the surrounding tape carries the context) and D3 (where the kicker is already a masthead bar). Risky on D1 and D4, where the kicker carries more of the framing load.
Recommendation

Ship D3 (The Broadsheet), with the quietly-factual kicker.

D3 is the only one of the four where every substance source is visible above the fold and none of them carries the page on its own. On the 51st Tuesday of "BTC Accumulation," column I refreshes with a new issue, column II refreshes with the day's tape, column III refreshes with whatever Wednesday holds. The cycle state, sat as a masthead bar above all three, is the only static element on the page — and that stability becomes its argument: the reading hasn't changed because the evidence hasn't asked it to.

The three-register typographic discipline (serif / mono tabular / mono dateline) is what defends it against feeling like a marketing page. Each column looks like what it is. None of them is pretending to be a card. None of them is decoration. The roman-numeral column heads — I, II, III — are the editorial gesture that solves "feels basic" without adding components.

Pair with the quietly-factual kicker (CURRENT CALL · BTC ACCUMULATION · HELD SINCE TUE 25 MAR 2026) because a masthead bar wants a wire-service register, not a literary one. The "held since" date is the move: it's a fact a reader can verify, it ages well, and it lets a reader see how long a state has held without the page having to perform the duration with a counter.

Fallback: if three columns at 1180px proves too tight typographically, D2 (The Tape) is the right retreat — it carries the same evidence-led posture in a more forgiving two-column body, and its twelfth-month-test score is arguably the strongest of the four. D1 (Front Page) is the right answer only if you decide the writing should be the product's calling card. D4 (Interleaved Lede) is the most interesting, but it needs a curator's habit you don't yet have. Ship D3 first; revisit D4 once the per-issue pull-selection workflow proves cheap.

I'm not proposing a Direction E. D's underlying thesis — build the homepage out of things that refresh on their own clocks — is right, and the four layouts above cover the meaningful theory-space on it. The choice between them is a choice between which source leads, not which thesis to back.
