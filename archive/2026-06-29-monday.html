Chart Spotlight · 01
US Dollar Index

DXY traded near 101.33 on 29 June, up roughly 2.1% over four weeks from about 99.21 on 29 May, and it is the single most important chart on the board this week — the lead headwind that is now dragging real yields and Global M2 with it. The index has reclaimed and held the 100 line cleanly, with the rate-differential bid rebuilt after the Fed's hawkish June hold.

The level to watch is 100.00. A daily close back below rotates real yields lower and pulls Global M2 back toward supportive, lifting the dial's net from +1 toward +3. A sustained hold above 101 keeps both dollar-linked headwinds live; a push through 102 on a hot jobs print is the configuration that risks a third indicator and the "at risk of flipping" band.

Thursday's jobs report is the pivot. A soft print rotates DXY off 101 in the same session; a strong one invites the 102 retest. Watching the dollar index intraday Thursday is the cleanest way to see the dial's score move in real time.

Chart Spotlight · 02
BTC

BTC enters the week with dominance near 55.8%, just above the sub-55% threshold that would rotate the cycle toward Alt Rotation — the level that matters more than price for the dial this week. Spot funding at +0.01%/8h and a market cap holding around $2.16T describe a market that is consolidating rather than trending, with positioning rebuilt off the earlier de-leveraged washout but breadth still narrow.

The read is positional. A hold above the 55% dominance line keeps the dial squarely in BTC Accumulation; a decisive break below, alongside a stablecoin-dominance fall, would be the mechanism — not a price rally alone — that carries the cycle into Alt Rotation. Neither has triggered.

BTC's price is increasingly correlated with the same dollar-and-real-yield read that runs through every other indicator, so the dollar remains the cleaner tell. Watch dominance at 55%, and watch DXY at 100.

Chart Spotlight · 03
ETFFLOWS — long-term context

Spot Bitcoin ETF flows sit beneath the scorecard as the deeper read on marginal demand, and the tape has cooled from the relentless green runs that defined the product's first eighteen months. Through 2026 the pattern has turned mixed — smaller inflow days interspersed with genuine outflow sessions — and BTC has drifted sideways as the structural bid thinned into the second half.

The signal is honest about the current setup. Flows are not one of the eight scorecard indicators, but they sit alongside BTC funding (supportive, +0.01%) and stablecoin dominance (neutral, 14.4%) as the positioning layer under the dial. There is no capitulation cluster and no panic outflow streak — the marginal allocator has stepped back, not away.

The tie to the dial is direct. A return to multi-week sustained inflows would be the leading tell that conviction is rebuilding and the net can climb back above +2. A sustained outflow run, stacked on the dollar's existing headwind, is the configuration that would put real pressure on the BTC Accumulation phase. Thursday's jobs report is the macro catalyst most likely to rotate the flow picture in either direction.
