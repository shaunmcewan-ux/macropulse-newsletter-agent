Chart Spotlight · 01
BTC — this week’s setup

BTC remains inside the broader accumulation range, but the short-term trend is bearish. The $68,400 pivot — the late-April breakout and first-test support — failed cleanly in the Asian session, with the tape printing $65,708 intraday. Until BTC prints a higher low against this morning's flush, the bearish near-term structure is intact.

The line we are watching is the long-term trendline at roughly $63,000, which has held the cycle since the November base. A close above this level, followed by a higher low on the next pullback, is the reversal pattern that ends the bearish near-term trend and reopens the path higher.

Below it, the structural floor is the previous low at around $60,000. That level has to hold. A weekly close below $60k invalidates the accumulation read and opens the door to another leg down toward the $55–58k zone, where cycle conviction would be re-evaluated.

For now: we sit in the accumulation range with the trend short-term bearish. We are watching for the higher low.

mp-card-spot2
Chart Spotlight · 02
BTC.D — cross-asset context

BTC.D broke out of a multi-week wedge pattern to the upside earlier in May, pushing dominance above 56% on the back of relative BTC strength and alt weakness. That breakout was consistent with the BTC Accumulation phase we have been calling — capital concentrating in BTC, the long tail underperforming.

The picture has now changed at the margin. Dominance has broken back down from the wedge highs and is currently trading near 59%, with the recent action carving a lower high and an early break of the up-trend. If this breakdown extends — confirmed by a sustained move back below 58% and then 56% — it is the first technical signal that capital is beginning to leak from BTC into the long tail.

That would be the early read on Alt Rotation forming, even before the macro confirms. We are monitoring whether the breakdown holds. Until it does, the BTC Accumulation phase remains intact and the dial does not move.
