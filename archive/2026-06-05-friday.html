Chart Spotlight · 01
ISM PMI — macro tape extends its run

ISM Manufacturing PMI printed 54.0 for May, a jump of +1.3 from April and the fifth consecutive month above the 50 expansion threshold. The chart shows a clean break of the multi-year downtrend that capped the index from mid-2022 through late-2024, with the reading now sitting above all four major moving averages and the MAs themselves curling upward.

The macro read is positive. A sustained run above 50 — particularly with this kind of slope — historically aligns with periods where risk assets get the benefit of the doubt: rising new orders feed forward into earnings and capex, the dollar tends to soften as global growth catches up, and crypto correlates with the broader risk regime rather than fighting it. The fifth straight month above 50 takes this from a bounce to a confirmed trend.

The catalyst to watch is the next monthly print on 1 July. A sixth consecutive expansion reading — especially if the new orders and employment sub-components join in — would reinforce the macro tailwind heading into the back half of the year and would be the second consecutive supportive signal from this indicator within the scorecard.

Chart Spotlight · 02
10Y−2Y curve — testing the lower trendline

The 10Y−2Y curve printed +0.42% this week and is now testing the lower trendline of a tight multi-month wedge. The chart shows the spread breaking down through the rising support line that has held since the November steepening began, with the 50-day rolling over and the 200-day flattening. This is the cleanest technical breakdown on the curve since the inversion phase ended.

A confirmed close below the trendline — and below the recent low at +0.41% — would signal that the curve is rolling back toward flat or inverted. That matters because the steepening trend was the macro tape's cleanest "growth coming, cuts coming" signal. Losing it argues the market is pricing the opposite: a higher-for-longer Fed, growth uncertainty, and term-premium compression at the long end.

This week's NFP at +172K — well above the +115K April print — was the catalyst. The strong labor number pushed June cut probability back below 20% and the curve responded by giving back the post-CPI steepening. We are watching whether the trendline holds on a closing basis; a break-and-hold below would shift the curve indicator's status next week.
