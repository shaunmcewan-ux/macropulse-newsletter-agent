mp-card-spot1
Chart Spotlight · 01
US Dollar Index

DXY printed 100.76 on Friday and is back inside the headwind band the dial scored against the cycle through mid-May. The chart shows the index reclaiming the 100 line cleanly on the post-FOMC bid, with the 50-day curling back upward and the 200-day still flat — the rate-differential view that drove May's hot-CPI-cold-dollar paradox has reversed as the ECB's hike fully priced and the Fed's dot-plot resolved hawkish-of-expectations.

The technical level to watch is 100.50. A daily close back below opens the path to a retest of June's 99.50 lows and pulls the indicator back to neutral; a sustained hold above pushes the next target to 101.80, where DXY rejected in early April. Above 101.80 and the headwind hardens — the dollar starts pulling real yields with it, and the dial's net score compresses toward +1.

PCE is the pivot. A cooler print rotates DXY off the 100.50–100.80 zone in the same session; a hotter print invites the 101.80 retest. Watching the dollar index intraday Thursday is the cleanest way to see the dial's score move in real time.

mp-card-spot2
Chart Spotlight · 02
BTC

BTC sits in the $63k–$68k accumulation range entering the week, having recovered off the diagonal trendline support that held through the June de-leveraged washout. The chart shows price back above the 50-day moving average but below the 200-day, with the short-term structure still printing lower highs since the May breakdown — the textbook condition for a range-bound consolidation rather than a directional move.

The level that matters is $65,500 — Friday's close — and below it the $63,000 long-term trendline that held the cycle since November. A daily close above $68,000 would print the higher low that breaks the lower-highs sequence and reopens the path to the $73k area; a weekly close below $63,000 would invalidate the broader accumulation read and force a re-evaluation of cycle conviction.

The setup is positional. PCE is the catalyst. The dial does not move on BTC's price alone, but BTC's price is increasingly correlated with the same dollar-and-real-yield read that runs through every other indicator. Watch the dollar; trade the chart.

mp-card-spot3
Chart Spotlight · 03
ETFFLOWS — long-term context

Spot Bitcoin ETF flows have lost their earlier dominance as the marginal driver. The chart shows the daily inflow/outflow tape that defined 2024 — extended runs of $500M-plus green days with limited red — has given way through 2026 to a mixed pattern: smaller inflow bars interspersed with notable outflow days. BTC price (yellow overlay) has tracked the change, drifting lower as the structural bid thinned through the second quarter.

The recent weeks tell the story underneath this month's chop. Outflows have featured prominently in the post-mid-June window, with multiple red days of meaningful magnitude alongside the de-leveraged washout. That is a different setup from the early-cycle period when ETF inflows continued straight through 6–10% spot drawdowns — the marginal allocator buyer has stepped back, not stopped, but the cleanest "calendar bid" signal that defined the first eighteen months of the product has weakened.

The implication for the dial is honest. ETF flows are not a scorecard indicator, but they sit beneath BTC funding (still supportive at +0.005%) and stablecoin dominance (neutral, 13.6%, dry powder building) as the deeper read on positioning. Today's tape says: the bid is thinner, but the regime has not flipped — there are no panic outflows, no capitulation cluster. A return to multi-week sustained inflows would be the leading signal that conviction is strengthening; a sustained outflow run alongside DXY's existing headwind is the configuration that would test the BTC Accumulation phase. PCE on Thursday is the macro catalyst that could rotate this picture in either direction.
