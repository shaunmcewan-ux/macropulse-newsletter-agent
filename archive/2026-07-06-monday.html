Chart Spotlight · 01
REAL10Y — this week’s setup

The US 10-year real yield — the TIPS-implied rate, DFII10 — sits at 2.25%, and it is the chart the whole fortnight routes through. It is one of the dial's two headwinds, and unlike the dollar it has a single dated release valve: June CPI on 14 July. The real yield has climbed roughly 20bp over four weeks, from about 2.05% in early June, and has held above the 2.00% line that separates a neutral read from a headwind since mid-June.

The level that matters is 2.00%. Above it, real yields are a sustained drag on equity and crypto risk premiums and the indicator's status stays headwind; a move below flips it to neutral and lifts the dial's net from +1 toward +2. The lever is inflation, not growth — ISM input prices collapsed 9.1pp to 73% in June, the largest monthly drop since 2022, which points to the pipeline relief a soft June CPI would confirm.

This is the cleanest single-variable read on the dial this fortnight. A June CPI that surprises soft pulls DFII10 toward and through 2.00% in the same session, and because the same disinflation drags the dollar with it, can retire both headwinds at once — the configuration that carries net back toward +3. A hot print entrenches the 2.25% level and keeps the dual headwind live into the 28–29 July FOMC.

Chart Spotlight · 02
BTC

BTC trades near $63,100 into the week, holding the lower half of the multi-week range that was consolidating on diagonal trendline support when this chart last appeared on 22 June. Since then a soft June payrolls print (+57K, unemployment 4.2%) failed to lift it: both dial headwinds held, and price drifted back toward the range floor rather than breaking higher. Dominance at 55.5% and a total market cap around $2.26T describe consolidation, not trend.

The read is that the diagonal support from the June spotlight is now being retested from above — into the CPI print rather than away from it. Spot funding at +0.01%/8h shows balanced leverage: no crowded long to flush, no capitulation. That leaves the next directional move likely to be macro-driven rather than positioning-driven.

The tie to the dial runs through dominance and the dollar, not the candle. A hold of the range low with BTC.D above 55% keeps the cycle squarely in BTC Accumulation; the mechanism that would change the state is a soft CPI rotating real yields and the dollar lower, not a price rally in isolation. Watch $63K as the range floor and the 14th as the catalyst.

Chart Spotlight · 03
MICA — long-term context

The thematic read this week is regulatory, and it shows up directly in the dial's stablecoin scorecard. The EU's MiCA regime hit its 1 July stablecoin deadline, and the reshuffle is visible in the CRYPTOCAP dominance tape: Tether's share (USDT.D) has rolled over from its late-June peak near 9% back to about 8.5%, while USDC.D has firmed near the top of its range around 3.4% — Circle's compliant coin gaining at Tether's expense as non-compliant USDT is removed from EU venues (Tether $186B USDT, Circle $75B USDC).

Stablecoin dominance is one of the eight scorecard indicators, and this week it reads supportive: total stablecoin dominance across all issuers has ground down to 13.63% of a $2.26T market, from the 14.3–14.5% range in early June, which the framework scores as capital rotating off the sidelines and into crypto. MiCA is a structural, one-off contributor to that shift rather than a pure risk-appetite signal, so the read carries a caveat — some of the move is compliance-driven redemption and venue migration, not fresh risk-taking. The direction still helps the dial; the cause is worth watching.

The tie to the dial is direct. A supportive stablecoin read is one of the three legs holding net at +1 — and it only stepped up to supportive this week, as ISM stepped down. If the MiCA-driven redemptions stabilise and dominance flattens, the indicator could drift back toward neutral and thin the supportive base further. For now the rotation is real, the dominance trend is down, and the leg holds.
