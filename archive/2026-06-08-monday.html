mp-card-spot1
Chart Spotlight · 01
BTC

The weekly chart is the correct lens here. After failing to hold above the $65,000–$68,000 range, BTC took a leg lower — but price found support precisely on the diagonal trend line running from the December 2022 low, the same structural reference flagged in last week's issue. This is the first meaningful test of that line since the bull market resumed, and the hold is significant: it means the broader structure remains intact.

What the weekly is now setting up is a potential double bottom reversal. The first bottom was established at the prior pivot low; the recent leg down and hold at the diagonal defines the second. The pattern is not confirmed until BTC delivers a weekly close back above the $65,000–$68,000 zone — that close would break the short-term lower-high sequence and set up a measured move back toward $80,000, the natural target for a double bottom of this depth and the level where the accumulation phase has historically transitioned toward strength.

The moving average picture supports patience: the 20-week is flattening near current price, and the 50-week continues to slope upward, acting as a trailing structural anchor. Weekly RSI has reset from overbought levels back to the mid-range — no momentum divergence, no distribution signal, just a clean consolidation against a well-defined trend line. The macro setup (BTC Accumulation at net +2, de-leveraged funding, $313B stablecoin dry powder) provides the external conditions for the bounce when the June 10 CPI and FOMC catalysts resolve.

mp-card-spot2
Chart Spotlight · 02
ETHBTC

ETH/BTC is the cycle tell this week. Despite BTC giving back ground and testing structural support, ETH held up with notable relative strength — a divergence that matters because weakness in alts during broad market stress is the norm; relative resilience is the exception and signals latent rotation appetite building under the surface.

On the weekly timeframe, ETHBTC is printing what appears to be a bull flag: a sharp preceding rally followed by a tight, orderly consolidation on contracting relative volume. The flag's pole established the directional intent; the current consolidation is the coiling phase before the next move. Bull flags on the weekly timeframe carry high conviction in cycle-aware frameworks — they tend to resolve in the direction of the preceding thrust.

The breakout target is the green zone on the chart, the area that historically aligns with Alt Season conditions — the phase where capital rotates broadly from BTC into the wider altcoin market. A confirmed weekly close above the flag's upper boundary in ETHBTC would be the first structural signal that Alt Rotation is forming, and that the dial's next phase is transitioning from accumulation into something broader. This does not change the current cycle call (BTC Accumulation remains intact), but BTC finding its double bottom and ETHBTC breaking its bull flag simultaneously would be the two-chart confirmation that the cycle is progressing on schedule.
