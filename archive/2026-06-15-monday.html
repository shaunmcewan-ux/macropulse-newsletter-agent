mp-card-spot1
Chart Spotlight · 01
BTC

BTC trades near $65,800, up roughly 4.5% over the past week and reclaiming ground after the early-June flush. Structure is constructive but not yet decisive: price is rebuilding above the mid-$60Ks with BTC dominance firming to 56.6% and the total market holding around $2.27T — a market led by Bitcoin rather than by broad risk appetite, which is exactly the signature of an accumulation phase.

The level that matters on the upside is the high-$60Ks into $70K, the supply shelf that has capped every rally since the spring. A weekly close above it, ideally on the back of a benign dot-plot, would be the first technical confirmation that the macro headwind is releasing. On the downside, the early-June low is the line that defines the range; funding at a barely-positive 0.0031% says leverage is not the risk here, so any flush would be macro-driven rather than a long squeeze.

The trade implication is to let Wednesday resolve. Funding this clean and dominance this firm argue against chasing into the FOMC; the asymmetry favours adding on a hold-with-cuts-retained reaction that pulls real yields toward 2%, and respecting the range low if the dot-plot turns hawkish.

mp-card-spot2
Chart Spotlight · 02
US Dollar Index

The US Dollar Index eased to ~99.56 to open the week, but the structure is the story: DXY has gained roughly 1.3% over the past month and sits just below its two-month high, leaving the 4-week trend pointed up even as today's tape ticks lower. The dial scores DXY neutral — it trades in the no-man's-land between the 97.5 level that would mark a genuine tailwind for risk and the 100 line that would tip it back to a headwind.

That 100 handle is the level to watch, and it is no accident the index is testing it into the FOMC. The dollar and real yields are the two macro variables holding the cycle's only headwind in place, and both resolve on the same catalyst. A dot-plot that retains 2026 cuts caps the dollar and likely sends DXY back toward the mid-98s, easing the backdrop; a hawkish strike of those cuts is the path to a clean break above 100 and a firmer dollar.

For crypto the read is direct. A dollar that stays under 100 keeps the liquidity floor intact and the BTC Accumulation setup unbothered; a decisive close above 100 would be the single most important signal that the macro headwind is broadening from real yields alone into the dollar as well. The Tuesday BOJ hike, by strengthening the yen, leans gently against that break — but Wednesday's SEP is what decides which side of 100 the dollar finishes the week.

Chart Spotlight · 03
Bitcoin Rainbow Chart

Step back from the week's noise and the long-term picture reinforces the dial. On the logarithmic rainbow band — Bitcoin's price plotted against its multi-year growth curves — price near $65,800 sits in the lower-middle bands, the blue-to-green "Fire sale / BUY / Accumulate / Still cheap" zone rather than the orange-and-red "FOMO / Sell" territory that has marked every prior cycle top.

This is the structural backdrop the weekly dial reads as BTC Accumulation. The rainbow is a slow indicator and should never be traded on its own, but it frames the tactical setup: the asset is in the part of its long-run range where accumulation has historically been rewarded, not where late-cycle distribution begins. A macro catalyst that releases the real-yield headwind would be acting on a market positioned low in its own band — the configuration in which constructive surprises tend to travel furthest.
